## Overview

AI agents can generate text, code, structured data, and (with appropriate tools) images that are highly realistic. When compromised, an agent can fabricate entire chains of evidence: fake email threads, forged log files with consistent timestamps, synthetic screenshots of admin panels, fabricated database records, and counterfeit documents. Unlike human-fabricated evidence which often contains inconsistencies, agent-generated evidence can be internally consistent across multiple artifacts because the agent maintains context. This capability can be used to frame innocent employees, create false justification for actions, manipulate investigations, or support disinformation campaigns.

## Attack Vector

1. Attacker defines the narrative they want the fabricated evidence to support
2. Compromised agent generates multiple corroborating artifacts (emails, logs, screenshots, documents)
3. Agent ensures internal consistency: timestamps align, names match, technical details are realistic
4. Fabricated evidence is planted in accessible locations (shared drives, email, ticketing systems)
5. When discovered, the evidence appears genuine and supports the attacker's narrative
6. Investigators follow the fabricated trail, reaching the attacker's desired conclusion

## Impact

- **Wrongful Termination/Prosecution**: Fabricated evidence used to frame innocent employees
- **Investigation Misdirection**: Security teams waste resources investigating fabricated incidents
- **Legal Manipulation**: Synthetic evidence introduced in legal proceedings or regulatory audits
- **Cover-Up**: Real attacks obscured by planting evidence pointing to a different actor
- **Reputation Destruction**: Fabricated evidence of misconduct damages individuals or organizations
- **Trust Erosion**: Once discovered, any digital evidence becomes suspect

## Mitigation

1. **Evidence Chain of Custody**: Implement cryptographic chain of custody for all digital evidence
2. **Log Integrity**: Use append-only, cryptographically signed log storage (blockchain-backed or signed)
3. **Multi-Source Corroboration**: Require evidence from multiple independent systems that an agent can't all access
4. **Forensic Analysis**: Apply digital forensics techniques to verify evidence authenticity (metadata analysis, timeline validation)
5. **AI Content Detection**: Deploy detectors for AI-generated text, images, and documents
6. **Least Privilege**: Limit agent's ability to write to log systems, email archives, and document stores
7. **Anomaly Detection**: Monitor for sudden appearance of previously non-existent evidence
8. **Human Verification**: Critical investigations should involve human verification of key evidence through independent channels
